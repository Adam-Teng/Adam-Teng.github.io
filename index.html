Here's some inline KaTeX: \(x^{2} + y^{2} = z^{2}\). It's wrapped in ``verbatim`` quotes. The paragraph continues on after the equation like this.

Here's some block-level KaTeX without any markup wrapping it:

$$ \int x^2 \cdot cos(x) \, dx $$

In general, you should wrap KaTeX markup in a raw block so the Syntext parser will ignore it.

Here's a block-level equation inside a ::: raw tag:

\begin{equation} 1 + 2 = 3 \end{equation}

And here's a block-level equation inside a :p raw tag:

\begin{equation} x + y = z \end{equation}

Here's a matrix inside a :p raw tag:

$$ \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{pmatrix} $$

The matrix won't render without the $$ wrappers, even though the KaTeX documentation says it should.

One final equation for luck, without any wrappers:

\begin{equation} e = m \cdot c^2 \end{equation}

And a final paragraph with no math markup.
